AIRPLANE

     

When World War I began, the airplanes quickly advanced and by the end of the war, 15,000 more planes were produced. Two aircraft companies, Boeing and Lockheed, became the leading companies to produce planes for the war. The first version of an airplane was first introduced by Orville and Wilbur Wright in 1903. Although the plane only flew for twelve seconds, this sparked many ideas to critique the airplane. The French created an aero-engine in 1905, but by this time, the Wright brothers had been able to travel in their newly built airplane for over three hours. The airplane was first used in the Italo-Turkish War in 1911 and 1912. Airplanes dropped explosives on their enemy's territories which created a great quantity of destruction. At the time, the airplanes were not exceptionally reliable because they couldn’t carry heavy materials and were dangerous. This weapon changed the nature of war because it sped up the delivery of mail, caused a tremendous amount of damage to countries, and it helped gather information on enemies. Countries could communicate with one another quicker and much more efficiently. When the planes became stronger, they could carry heavier machinery and drop bombs on their opponents to cause mass destruction. The airplanes could travel over land to track down enemy bases and see what they’re planning. Now, airplanes are used for leisure for people, and are also used for the military. Fighter airplanes are equipped with missiles for fighting other planes, and a lot of technology to detect enemy aircrafts.

     

     

     

HOW IT WORKS

     

During World War One, airplanes were still a relatively new technology, having only been invented a few years prior. Nevertheless, they quickly became a vital component of military strategy, serving primarily as reconnaissance aircraft to gather intelligence on enemy positions and movements. Early airplanes were made of wood and fabric and were powered by internal combustion engines, which allowed them to achieve speeds of up to 100 miles per hour. They were also armed with machine guns and bombs, which enabled them to attack ground targets and engage in aerial combat with enemy planes. Pilots flew the planes using basic instruments, such as compasses and altimeters, and relied on their skill and experience to navigate through often dangerous and unpredictable conditions. Overall, airplanes played a crucial role in World War One, helping to revolutionize warfare and paving the way for the modern military aircraft that we know today.
